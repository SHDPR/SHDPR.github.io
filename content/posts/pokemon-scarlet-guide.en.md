If you're new to the Pokemon series or returning after a long break, Scarlet/Violet is a great entry point.
It's the first mainline title to feature a fully open world, which is exciting — but that freedom can also leave newcomers wondering where to start.
This guide covers everything you need to know to get going in Scarlet.

---

## Scarlet vs Violet — What's the Difference?

The core gameplay is identical between the two versions, but there are meaningful differences in Pokemon availability and story flavor.

| Category | Scarlet | Violet |
|---|---|---|
| Legendary | Koraidon | Miraidon |
| Professor | Professor Sada | Professor Turo |
| Paradox Pokemon | Ancient forms (e.g. Great Tusk, Flutter Mane) | Future forms (e.g. Iron Treads, Iron Bundle) |
| Exclusive theme | Ancient / past | Mechanical / future |

**Recommendation for newcomers: Scarlet.** The ancient-form Paradox Pokemon have straightforward stat distributions, and Koraidon functions as a reliable physical attacker that's easy to use once you have it.

## Choosing Your Starter

| Pokemon | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sprigatito → Meowscarada | Grass / Dark | Wide move pool, strong late game |
| Fuecoco → Skeledirge | Fire / Ghost | **Best for beginners** — Ghost typing nullifies Normal and Fighting moves |
| Quaxly → Quaquaval | Water / Fighting | Great offensive coverage with Water + Fighting |

**Go with Fuecoco.** Fire is favored or at worst neutral against most gym leaders, and once it evolves into Skeledirge, the Ghost typing makes it immune to Normal and Fighting-type moves — a huge safety net when you're still learning.

## Recommended Gym Order

Scarlet/Violet is open world, meaning you can tackle gyms in any order. But gym leaders scale to fixed levels, so tackling them out of order can make things unnecessarily difficult. Here's the smoothest route:

1. **Cortondo Gym (Bug — Katy)** — Level 14–15, do this first
2. **Artazon Gym (Grass — Brassius)** — Level 16–17
3. **Levincia Gym (Electric — Iono)** — Level 20–21
4. **Cascarrafa Gym (Water — Kofu)** — Level 26–27
5. **Medali Gym (Normal — Larry)** — Level 35–36
6. **Montenevera Gym (Ghost — Ryme)** — Level 41–42
7. **Alfornada Gym (Psychic — Tulip)** — Level 44–45
8. **Glaseado Gym (Ice — Grusha)** — Level 47–48

## Gameplay Features Worth Knowing

**Let's Go (Auto Battle)**
Press R to send your Pokemon out to automatically battle nearby wild Pokemon. Excellent for grinding levels without manual input. It will also pick up items automatically, so be mindful of your bag.

**Picnics**
Set up a picnic and make sandwiches to temporarily boost encounter rates for specific Pokemon types, or speed up egg hatching. Stock up on ingredients — the buffs are surprisingly useful.

**Mass Outbreaks**
When you see a cluster of the same Pokemon icon on the map, that's a Mass Outbreak. Chain battling in an outbreak increases your odds of finding a Shiny or high-stat Pokemon significantly.

**Terastallize**
Once per battle, you can Terastallize your Pokemon to change its type and power up moves of that type. Save it for gym leaders and tough fights — it's a reliable comeback tool when things get tight.

## Three Storylines Running in Parallel

Scarlet/Violet has three independent story paths you can tackle in any order:

- **Victory Road** — Defeat 8 gym leaders and challenge the Pokemon League. The classic path.
- **Starfall Street** — Take down 5 Team Star bases. Easier battles overall, good for EXP farming.
- **Path of Legends** — Hunt down 5 Titan Pokemon. The most narratively significant route.

Playing all three in parallel is the most efficient way to keep your levels balanced throughout the game.

Don't overthink the party composition on your first run. The best team is one you actually enjoy using.
Scarlet has no shortage of strong and visually interesting Pokemon — just catch what catches your eye and figure the rest out as you go.
